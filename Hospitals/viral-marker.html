HIV I & II – Screening Serum Non-Reactive Immunochromatography
  • This is a screening test only. All samples detected reactive must be confirmed by Western Blot or PCR
  • False positive test results are seen in Leprosy, renal disorders, TB, HSV, Hypergamma globulinemia, RA, ANA, Lipemic serum
  • Although a positive result may indicate infection with HIV-1 or HIV-2 virus, a diagnosis of AIDS can only be made on clinical grounds
  • A negative result does not eliminate the possibility of HIV-1/ HIV-2 Infection. The specimen may contain low levels of antibodies.
HCV – Screening Serum Non-Reactive Immunochromatography
  • This is only a screening test. All reactive samples should be tested by an ELISA confirmatory test.
HBsAg – Screening Serum Non-Reactive Immunochromatography
  • The test is validated for the detection of HBsAg in serum or plasma only and not in other body fluids.
  • This is only a screening test. All reactive samples should be confirmed by confirmatory test like PCR. The results of the test must be interpreted in relation to the clinical data.
  • False positive result can be obtained due to presence of elevated RF factor.
